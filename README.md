🎓 Student Academic Portal – Limkokwing Sierra Leone

## 📘 Project Overview
The **Student Academic Portal** is a GUI-based Python application developed for the **Principles of Structured Programming (PROG103)** final project at Limkokwing University Sierra Leone.  
It provides a digital solution for managing student records, courses, and results, aligned with **SDG 4 – Quality Education**.

---

## 🎯 Objectives
- Apply structured programming principles in a GUI environment.
- Design modular programs using functions and logical flow.
- Combine GUI components with backend program logic.
- Manage academic data using SQLite3 database.
- Publish and manage the project using GitHub.
- Deliver a solution aligned with Sierra Leone’s educational needs.

---

## ⚙️ Features
- 🔑 **Login Page** – Secure access with demo credentials (`admin / admin123`).  
- 📊 **Dashboard** – Displays live statistics (students, enrolments, courses, GPA).  
- 📝 **Student Registration** – Add or update student records with validation.  
- 📂 **Student Records** – Search and filter student data.  
- 📚 **Course Management** – Add and manage courses.  
- 🎓 **Result Processing** – Automate grade calculation and GPA assignment.  
- 📑 **Reports & Analytics** – Generate academic reports and performance insights.

 PROJECT RATIONALE
 Problem Description
Higher education institutions in Sierra Leone face persistent challenges in managing student academic records. Many universities still rely on manual, paper-based systems for enrollment, course registration, grade processing, and reporting. This outdated approach creates several critical issues:
 Data Loss & Inaccuracy Paper records are vulnerable to damage, misplacement, and duplication errors. This often results in incomplete or unreliable student information, making it difficult to track academic progress accurately.
 Administrative Delays Lecturers and administrators spend excessive time manually recording grades, preparing reports, and verifying student records. This reduces efficiency and limits the time available for teaching and student support.
Limited Accessibility Students frequently face delays in accessing their academic results and records. The lack of transparency undermines trust in the system and negatively impacts student motivation.
 Inefficient Reporting Generating accurate academic reports across departments is time-consuming and error-prone. Late submissions hinder timely decision-making and academic planning.
Lack of Integration Different departments often maintain separate records, leading to inconsistencies and duplication of effort. Without a centralized system, collaboration and data sharing are limited.
Impact on Quality of Education These inefficiencies reduce the overall quality of education delivery, making it harder for institutions to meet international standards and align with SDG 4 Quality Education.

System Explanation
The Student Academic Portal is a GUI-based desktop application developed in Python (tkinter). It provides a structured, digital solution to the challenges of academic record management in Sierra Leone.
How the Portal Solves the Issues
 Data Loss & Inaccuracy → Centralized Digital Records All student and course information is stored in structured Python data structures (lists/dictionaries). This reduces duplication errors and ensures records are consistent and retrievable.
 Administrative Delays → Automated Processes Grade calculation, GPA computation, and report generation are automated using decision structures and loops, saving lecturers and administrators valuable time.
Limited Accessibility → Searchable Interface Students and administrators can quickly search and filter records by name, ID, or department. Results are displayed in paginated tables for easy navigation.
 Inefficient Reporting → Instant Reports The Reports module generates academic reports with GPA breakdowns and department statistics. Export functionality (PDF in future versions) ensures reports are shareable and professional.

 SDG Relevance
The Student Academic Portal directly supports the United Nations Sustainable Development Goal 4 (SDG 4: Quality Education), which aims to “ensure inclusive and equitable quality education and promote lifelong learning opportunities for all.”
Alignment with SDG 4
Inclusive Access: By digitizing academic records, the portal ensures that students across all departments have equal access to their academic information.
Efficiency & Transparency: Automated grade calculation and instant reporting reduce administrative delays, making education management more transparent and accountable.
Equitable Opportunities: Students can reliably track their progress, reducing the risk of bias or errors in manual record-keeping.
Capacity Building: The system empowers lecturers and administrators with tools to manage academic data more effectively, freeing time for teaching and student support.
Digital Transformation in Education: By replacing paper-based systems with a structured, GUI-based solution, the portal helps Sierra Leone leapfrog traditional infrastructure challenges and align with global education standards.
Broader Impact
Governance: Supports institutional accountability through accurate reporting.
Innovation: Encourages adoption of open-source digital public goods in education.
Scalability: Can be extended to other universities, contributing to national education reform.
